Help- A quick guide to this dashboard

This dashboard uses interactive charts to display a data set on London road casualties between 2010-2014. Most of the charts can filtered to specific data by clicking the sections that display data, this will show across all the charts. For example if you click 'Female' in the first pie chart all the charts will show only data for females. Or if you click Ealing on the bar chart you will only see the data for Ealing across all charts. If you click multiple sections you will see combined data so if you had clicked Ealing and then Islington in the bar char you will see combined results across the charts.

Please note: the first number display showing the number of incidents 2010-2014 will not change and the 2nd number display showing percentage of severe and fatal incidents are not clickable but will change when the charts are clicked to reflect that specific data set. Clicking the line chart will also not change the displays across other charts but the data on that chart itself will repond to clicking the other charts.

To reset the data at anytime click the 'Reset' button at the top right of the screen in the main navigation bar, this will be visible as you scroll down the page.

The data was sourced from London Datastore, some rows of data that were not necassary for this dashboard were removed, to see the original data see the page Road Casualties by Severity.

London Road Casualties 2010-2014

Welcome to this dashboard that displays a data set of casualties on the roads of London between 2010-2014. This range of displays and charts will give you an overview of the risks factors to look out for on London's roads if your a driver, passenger or pedestrian.
You can gain an insight into keeping safe by filtering these interactive charts to see a more specific data set. Click on the section of the chart that you would like to see information for and all of the charts will then change to display that data. You can reset them at any time by clicking the 'Reset' button at the top right of the screen. For more details and examples click the 'Help' button at the top right of the screen.

No. of incidents between 2010-2014
13,206

London had a population of over 8 million and around 30 million visitors per year over this time period. You are fairly unlikely to be involved in a traffic incident even though the roads can get very busy.

Percentage of serious incidents

You are much more likely to have a serious injury if involved in a traffic incident in London rather than a fatal one. This could potentially be a life changing injury though.

Percentage of fatal incidents

Although the number of fatal incidents is low you should still stay vigilant when out and about on the streets of London. The percentage of fatal incidents increases to 7% when the data is filtered to just pedestrians.

Percentage of males and females involved in incidents

Males are more than twice as likely to be a casualty in a road accident in London. This is true acorss the UK with men accounting for 74% road traffic deaths and 70% of serious injuries according to the road safety charity Brake. According to their data, on average men also cover more than twice as many miles as women per year.

Percentage of casualties in each age range invloved in incidents

Teenagers and young adults are the most likely to be involved in an incident with more than half of incidents involving 13-38 year olds.

Percentage of casulaties per casualty class involved in incidents

Drivers and riders are more likely to be casualties in the incidents. Pedestrians are about 20% less likely to be involved in an incident compared to drivers and riders for the whole data set. The risk for both pedestrians and passengers increases with certain vehicles are involved.

Number of incidents per areas of London

This chart will give you an idea of what areas have the most incidents. You can click on the individual bars for a certain area to filter the other charts show details for just that area.

Westminster and Lambeth are quite risky areas perhaps due to the fact that they have a high tourist footfall as well has many businesses and offices. Many central areas are very busy most of the day and with so many pedestrians, cyclists and general road users trying to get somewhere, often in a hurry, it is no surprise accidents will happen.

Composite line chart of the time of day incidents occur

This chart will give you an insight of what time of the day an accident is most likely to happen. Hover over each day of the week in the key to the top, left of the chart to focus on the line for that particular day. Hover over the lines, inline with a hour, to see the number of incidents that occured at that time.

As you would expect there is a spike in incidents that occur during the evening rush hours Monday to Friday as many people are trying to get home. There is a small spike during the morning rush too but the streets and roads get much more congested throughout the day.

Row chart of vehicles types involved in incidents

Check if your choice of transport is a risky one with this chart, certain methods of travel are obviously more vunerable than others. It can be hard for some vehicles to keep an eye out for pedestrians, pedal bike and moter bikes weaving through traffic. Some vehicles are split by age such as 'Car (from 2005)' is 'Car' which is cars made after 2005. Hover over each bar to see the total for each vehicle type.

Cars are by far the vehicle most likely to be involved in an incident. However pedal cycles are the most likly in quite a few different area of London. Collectively the various sized motorcyles are another vehicle that is likely to be involved in an accident.
