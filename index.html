London Road Casualties 2010-2014

    • Help- A quick guide to this dashboard

    This dashboard uses interactive charts to display a data set on London road casualties between 2010-2014. Each chart can filtered to specific data by clicking on it, this will show across all the charts. For example if you click 'Female' in the first piechart all the charts will show only data for females. Or if you click Ealing on the bar chart you will only see the data for Ealing across all charts. If you click multiple sections you will see combined data so if you had clicked Ealing and then Islington in the bar char you will see combined results across the charts.

    Please note: the first number display showing the number of incidents 2010-2014 will not change and the 2nd number display showing percentage of severe and fatal incidents are not clickable but will change when the charts are clicked to reflect that specific data set.

    To reset the data at anytime click the reset button at the top right of the screen in the main navigation bar, this will be visible as you scroll down the page.

    The data was sourced from London Datastore, some rows of data that were not necassary for this dashboard were removed, to see the original data see the page Road Casualties by Severity.

This dashboard displays a data set of casualties on the roads of London between 2010-2014. The data includes all age groups and both severe and fatal incidents. This set of charts and data will help give you an idea of the safest areas on London to be on the road or a pedestrians. It will give you an idea of areas to avoid at what time.

No. of incidents 2010-2014
13,206

London had a population of over 8 million and around 30 million visitors per year over this time period. You are unlikely to be involved in a traffic incident even though the roads can get very busy.

Percentage of incidents that are serious

You are much more likely to have a serious injury if in volved in a traffic incident in London rather than a fatal one. This could potentially be a life changing injury though.

Percentage of incidents that are fatal

Although the number of fatal incidents is low you should still stay vigilant when out and about on the streets of London.

Percentage of males and females involved in incidents

Males are more than twice as likely to be a casualty in a road accident in London. This is true acorss the UK with men accounting for 74% road traffic deaths and 70% of serious injuries according to the road safety charity Brake. According to their data, on average men also cover more than twice as many miles as women per year.

Percentage of casualties in each age range

Teenagers and young adults are the most likely to be involved in an incident with more than half of incidents involve 13-38 year olds.

Percentage of casulaties per casualty class involved in incidents

Drivers and riders are more likely to be casualties in the incidents, pedestrians are about 20% less likely to be involved in an incident. You will be safest as a passenger.
